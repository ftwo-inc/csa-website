About us

To make Career information accessible and actionable for people, education provider business and local authorities.

CSA is free to use (forever for individuals), with subscriptions for advanced features (refer to Subscriber Agreement). The high one-off fees used by recruiters encourage poor practice. Through transparent and fair pricing and a model that promotes high-quality matching, we want to make it faster, smoother and easier to find the right opportunity for you.

Ethics

We strive to build privacy respectful services that helps you create the career you want now and in the future - central element within our mission. We want to make everything transparent and usable.

We want to achieve our mission in the most Ethical way possible and challenge current industry approaches, such as how the Data (CV) is retained/used as people are contacted months or even years later.

How do we go about this

  1. All business have to be approved on our platform, this is to ensure that no recruitment agencies are able to use this, even if they wanted to pay.
  2. An individual can share their profile (data) with anyone by creating a share link, which can then be shared. You can then remove this link
  1. We won't use any of your data for marketing (unless you have consented) or sell to a third party.
  2. We set your profile as private when you first set it up, controlling the status of your profile (CV)- You can change this at any time.
    1. Private - No one can see this, you don't appear in any Talent pool or search
    2. Public - You are open on the platform and internet (check)
    3. Incognito - You appear in searches but personal details name, location, pictures are removed or replaced with a default one. Employers then need to request access to see your, you can approve and reject this.

Your Rights

YOUR RIGHTS AND CHOICES OVER YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION

U.K. Data protection law gives you a number of rights over your personal information such as a right to be informed and to access your information, a right to object to direct marketing and certain other processing, a right to rectify inaccurate information, and a right to erase information to name but a few.

Please see below for an explanation of your rights.

Please notify us of any changes to the personal information by maintaining your profile/setting within the application. If you are unable to do this then contact us using the details below.

You can contact us at any time by email:

  • sending an email to [email protected]. (please remember email is not a secure way of communicating sensitive information)

Right to know and right to access

You can ask us if we are holding and using information relating to you and if we are, to ask us:

  • why were processing your personal information
  • the categories of data we process about you
  • the recipients or categories of recipients of your data
  • how long we keep your information or the criteria we use to determine how long we need to keep it
  • for a copy of personal information, we hold about you

Right to rectify

You can ask us to rectify inaccuracies in your personal information. You can also ask us to complete personal information considered incomplete or to record a supplementary statement about the information.

Right to erasure

In some circumstances, you can ask us to erase personal information we hold about you. This includes when:

  • the information is no longer necessary in relation to the purpose for which it was collected and explained in our privacy notice
  • if you previously gave consent to the use of your information, but decide to withdraw it and we cannot justify another legal ground for using it under data protection law
  • we process your information based on our legitimate interests and we cannot demonstrate overriding legitimate grounds to continue processing the information
  • we dont have a lawful ground under data protection law to process your information
  • the data have to be erased to comply with a legal requirement

Right to restrict processing

You have the right to ask us to restrict (block or suppress) the processing of your personal information when:

  • you dispute the accuracy of the information (while we verify matters)
  • the processing is unlawful, and you object to the erasure of the information and request that we restrict processing instead
  • we no longer need the data, but you require it to establish, exercise or defend a legal claim
  • we process your information for our legitimate business interests but you object and while we verify the grounds for continued processing

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive personal information you provide to us, in a commonly used machine-readable format, but only when the processing of your information is:

  • based on your consent or where it is necessary for the performance of a contract, and
  • when the information is processed by solely by automated means

Right to object

Based on your particular situation, you can object to the processing of your personal information, that is:

  • based on our legitimate business interests (including profiling)
  • done for research and statistical purposes

You also have the right to object to the use of your personal information for direct marketing purposes (including profiling).

Right to withdraw consent

When we rely on your consent to process your personal information such as for sales and marketing communications you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. Well always strive to make it easy for you to withdraw consent, and if you find this isnt the case, then just email us as per below, and well try to fix things ASAP.

Rights related to automated decision making, including profiling

Data protection law seeks to safeguard individuals against harm that may arise from decision-making - including profiling - that takes place without human intervention. You have the right not to be subject to a decision - including profiling - when it is based on the automated processing of your personal information and it has a legal effect or a similarly significant effect on you.

Please note that the right does not apply when the processing is:

  • necessary for entering into or for the performance of a contract with you, or
  • when it is authorised by law, or
  • when it is based on your explicit consent
